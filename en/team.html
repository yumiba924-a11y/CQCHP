Toyoharu Tsutsui
Chairman, Representative Director

Born in 1951 in Fukuoka, Japan. Built his career at Nomura Securities, Morgan Stanley, and First Boston (where he served as Tokyo Branch Manager and Japan Representative), before establishing his own firm in 1996. For over 30 years he has led an independent financial holding company, and at CQC continues to be involved in final decision-making on outbound M&A by Japanese companies and inbound investment by foreign companies into Japan.

Wharton MBA · Harvard AMP · Over half a century of investment banking

Toyoharu Tsutsui led the acquisition of Tokyo Stock Exchange membership at two foreign investment banks and served as Tokyo Branch Manager and Japan Representative at First Boston Securities, before launching his independent investment banking business on July 4, 1996. Over 30 years since establishment, he has built the firm into an independent financial holding company comprising six operating subsidiaries — spanning securities, asset management, M&A advisory, PE investment, and a Vietnam local subsidiary — and continues to oversee the entire group's management today.

Co-founding member of Global Alliance Partners (GAP), an international cross-border M&A alliance. Established in Hong Kong in 2008, GAP now spans more than 20 countries of independent investment banks. The alliance forms the foundation for delivering neutral, cross-border solutions — uniquely available from an independent firm — for outbound M&A by Japanese companies and inbound investment by foreign companies into Japan.

Notable achievements at Nomura Securities include leading the 1981 Singapore IPO of Isetan (the first Singapore listing by a Japanese company, with a base of 2,500 individual shareholders) and securing the IPO mandate for Jurong Shipyard (IHI Group). Post-independence, key milestones include the 2003 acquisition of Prudential Life's Japan-based securities subsidiary (the launch of Capital Partners Securities), the successful execution of one of Japan's largest block trades — JPY 152.9 billion of Japanese equities — in March 2006, the formation in December 2006 of Japan's first Vietnam-focused fund (JPY 30 billion), and the launch of Capital Asset Management in 2010.

External roles: Chairman of the Japan-Vietnam Cultural Association (April 2006 – present, approx. 20 years); Director of the Noda Ikuei Foundation (public-interest); Member of the Kojunsha Society; Member of the Tokyo Rotary Club.

Hiroaki Hasegawa
General Manager, Investment Research Department

Graduated from Keio University, Faculty of Business and Commerce. Joined Nomura Investment Advisory's Systems Promotion Office in 1986 and spent approximately 39 years in quantitative asset management at Nomura Group. Held positions including Head of Quantitative Investment at Nomura Asset Management Toshin and Senior Portfolio Manager in the Product Development and Advisory Investment teams. As one of Japan's leading practitioners of multi-asset quantitative model management, he supports the analytical foundation behind CQC's investment decisions, deal analysis, and fund formation.

Keio Univ. · CMA · IT Passport · Career in quantitative management at Nomura Group

Hiroaki Hasegawa is CQC's head of investment research, having spent approximately 39 years in Nomura Group's asset management division — including pioneer work in quantitative modeling and advisory investment — since joining Nomura Investment Advisory's Systems Promotion Office, a pioneer of quantitative management in Japan, in 1986.

From 1986 through 2001, over 15 years at Nomura Investment Advisory's Systems Promotion Office, he engaged in quantitative research, quantitative management, and overseas business alliances. His coverage spanned equity stock-selection models (alpha forecasting at the individual-stock level), asset allocation models (multi-asset allocation), foreign exchange models, and commodity models. He was at the core of the early adoption in Japan of quantitative management techniques imported from the US in the late 1980s.

In 2001, he was appointed Head of Quantitative Investment (concurrently Senior Portfolio Manager) at Nomura Asset Management Toshin. From 2004, as Senior Portfolio Manager in Nomura Asset Management's Product Development team, he led the structuring and management of new products for nine years. From 2013, as Senior Portfolio Manager in the Advisory Investment team, he oversaw bespoke investment advisory and dedicated mandates for pension funds and institutional investors over 12 years.

He brings to CQC his deep experience in long-term quantitative investment, new-product development, and institutional advisory at one of Japan's largest asset management firms — as the foundation supporting the quality of CQC's investment decisions, deal analysis, and fund formation.
